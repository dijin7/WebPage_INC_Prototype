Nutrition for all

Soy is a vital source of food for humans as well as for animals. For people, soy is used in products like tofu, tempeh, soy milk, and other highly nutritious foods. Soy is rich in proteins, fiber, and essential fatty acids, which makes it a very important ingredient in vegan and vegetarian diets, but it is also consumed in diverse ways in many gastronomic cultures.

Regarding animal feed, soy is one of the principal components of said “feed” for livestock, birds, and fish. Providing a high protein content, it allows animals to grow faster and gain mass with better results and less time.

Our soybeans have genetic markers, which makes it so less money is spent in processing and the animals eat more as the soy has better digesting and tasting properties

Soy: very beneficial to the environment and population

The production of soy not only benefits those who consume it but also the environment. The variety of soy with yellow hilum (yellow stems) is particularly appreciated in the food industry because they have a high-quality grain and are very attractive for the farmers. This variety not only improves the nutritional value of the derived products but is also more friendly to the natural environment.
